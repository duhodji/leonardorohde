The Mechs Standoff

2024 | Adobe After Effects

In this video, made for a class on Video Graphics at BMCC, Leo explored the parallax effect to create an illusion of 3D depth and immersion by moving foreground elements faster than background elements, and the camera in Adobe After Effects. He started by making two stop motion animations of the Mechs (giant, humanoid or animal-like machine piloted from the inside) drawing their sword on a green screen using Gundam figurines. After that, he imported the videos and the images of the last two frames in After Effects to remove the greenscreen and add effects and sounds.

The eyes of the first Mech is a green rectangle placed behind the footage due to the fact that they were also green and disappeared during the greenscreen removal process. The lightsabers effects were made by using the lightning effect and turning down the forking and turbulence of the effect. For the camera, Leo used 3D Layers and placed the background and the first Mech, and all of its effects, back. The second Mech was placed on the foreground. The camera moved between the two. Most of the sound effects came from a game and one from an anime that Leo found while looking for sounds that fitted the animation.
